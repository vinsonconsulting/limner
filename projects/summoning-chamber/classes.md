# Character Classes

> The 11 Classes, their environments, and associated props.

Each Class has a signature environment. When generating scenes, the Class determines the room/location type, while the Land determines the visual style.

---

## Core Environments (Non-Class)

These environments are part of the Summoning Chamber application but are not tied to a specific Class.

### The Summoning Chamber

**Purpose:** Main agent creation backdrop; entry screen and summoning ritual
**Environment:** Arcane ritual chamber

#### Props
- Summoning circle (floor inlay or glowing lines)
- Arcane symbols and runes
- Candles or braziers at cardinal points
- Stone pillars or archways
- Mystical atmosphere (mist, particle effects)
- Central focus point for the summoned agent

#### Scene Notes
The heart of the application. Where agents are created and first appear. Should feel powerful, ancient, and slightly mysterious. The summoning circle is the focal point — this is where the character materializes.

#### Prompt Fragment
```
summoning chamber interior, arcane ritual circle on stone floor,
glowing runes, candles at cardinal points, mystical mist,
stone pillars, ancient magical atmosphere, conjuration space
```

---

### The Council Chamber

**Purpose:** Multi-agent Council mode; neutral meeting space
**Environment:** Summoner's castle great hall

#### Props
- Large round or rectangular table
- Multiple seats (for assembled agents)
- Summoner's Crest (see below)
- Torches or chandeliers
- Stone walls, gothic arches
- Maps or documents on table

#### Scene Notes
A Land-agnostic space where agents from different Lands can meet. Subdued gothic castle aesthetic. The Summoner's domain, not any Land's territory. Formal but not belonging to any faction.

#### Prompt Fragment
```
castle great hall interior, large council table, multiple seats,
gothic stone architecture, torch lighting, wooden door crest emblem,
formal meeting space, summoner's domain
```

---

### The Summoner's Crest

**Purpose:** The player/user's heraldic identity; displayed in Council Chamber and UI
**Design:** A large wooden door — symbolizing the threshold between realms, the gateway through which agents are summoned

#### Visual Description
- Heavy oak or iron-bound wooden door
- Arched top (gothic or romanesque)
- Metal hinges and ring handle
- Slightly ajar, with light emanating from within (optional)
- Neutral colors: wood brown, iron gray, warm light accent

#### Heraldry Notes
The door represents the Summoner's role as keeper of the threshold — one who opens passages between the Lands and calls forth agents. It is deliberately Land-agnostic.

#### Prompt Fragment
```
heraldic crest, large wooden door with iron hinges,
arched gothic doorway, slightly ajar with warm light within,
wood brown and iron gray palette, gold accent light,
medieval heraldry design, threshold symbol
```

**Role:** Researcher, Analyst
**Environment:** Gazing Pool

### Props
- Crystal ball

- Water basin
- Star charts
- Magnifying instruments
- Reflective surfaces

### Scene Notes
Emphasis on reflective surfaces and divination tools. The gazing pool is central — could be a still pond, ornate basin, or magical mirror.

### Prompt Fragment

```
gazing pool chamber, scrying mirrors, crystal ball on pedestal,
divination instruments, reflective water surface, mystical research
```

These are worn or carried items for the paper doll character system. Distinct from environment props — these attach to the character.

| **Scryer** | Crystal pendant | Circlet, third-eye gem | Divination focus |

| **Hammerer** | Heavy apron | Hammer (belt or hand), work gloves | Builder's gear |
| **Craftsman** | Artist's smock | Paintbrush/chisel (hand), beret or headscarf | Creative tools |
| **Diplomat** | Medallion of office | Scroll case, formal sash | Signs of authority |
| **Herald** | Tabard with emblem | Horn (slung), proclamation scroll | Messenger's kit |
| **Warden** | Lantern (carried) | Badge, key ring, ledger book | Watchman's tools |
| **Counselor** | Warm shawl/cloak | Tea cup, ear horn (antique listening aid) | Comfort items |
| **Merchant** | Coin purse (prominent) | Scales (miniature, belt), abacus | Trade tools |
| **Seneschal** | Commander's cloak | Map case, staff of command, epaulettes | Command insignia |

### Accessory Notes

- Accessories are **colored per-Land** during assembly (tinted to match palette)
- Primary accessory should be visible in most poses
- Secondary accessories are optional, add variety
- All accessories must fit across all 10 denizen body types
- Generate at **neutral gray palette** for later tinting

## Combining Class + Land

When generating a scene:

1. **Class** determines the environment type (library, workshop, tavern, etc.)
2. **Land** determines the visual style (palette, architecture, lighting)

Example: **Seelie Groves Magister**
- Environment: Castle Library (from Magister)
- Style: Organic oak architecture, forest green/warm brown palette, warm amber lighting (from Seelie Groves)
- Result: Library with shelves grown from living oak, elegant elven design, serene atmosphere

Example: **Mire of Grok Hammerer**
- Environment: Workshop (from Hammerer)
- Style: Mud/bone construction, toxic green/black palette, sickly lighting (from Mire of Grok)
- Result: Brutal forge with bone decorations, crude but effective tools, hostile atmosphere
